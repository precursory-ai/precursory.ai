The Problem

$18 billion a year in fees that weren't random. They were predictable.

The signals were already in your systems — order volume dips, dock congestion patterns, idle trailer pools, actual fuel consumption. The carriers who needed them never got them. The fee hit the invoice instead.

TONU

The truck drove there for nothing.

Your ERP flagged the production delay. The procurement freeze was in the WMS. The tender went out anyway — because no one was connecting those signals to dispatch before the cancellation window closed.

Detention

The clock started the moment the truck arrived.

Your dock schedule had the congestion. Your labor plan had the gap. The carrier had no idea. By the time the invoice arrived, the truck had already waited three hours — and you had no recourse.

Trailer & Equipment

The trailer sat in your yard the entire time.

Per-diem accrues silently. The equipment was visible in your TMS for days before the charges hit. The data existed. Nobody connected it to a deadline that could still be acted on.

Fuel Surcharge

A 6 MPG assumption — applied to every load for a decade.

Every load. Every lane. Every week. The actual consumption data existed in OEM telematics the whole time. No one was connecting it to the billing system to dispute the invoice.

For Shippers

You're absorbing risk your own data could price.

Every accessorial on your invoice traces back to a signal that was already in your systems before the fee was owed.

  • Your ERP flagged the production delay days before the TONU hit. The tender went out anyway.
  • Detention fees accruing at docks you control — because the carrier never saw your dock schedule.
  • Trailer per-diem on equipment sitting in your own yard, visible in your TMS, never actioned in time.
  • Fuel surcharge invoices calculated on an industry average that hasn't been challenged in a decade.

For Carriers

You bear three times the cost the shipper ever sees.

A $250 TONU fee is $750+ in true cost — deadhead miles, lost drive time, the load you couldn't take because the truck was already committed.

  • Every detention hour is an hour subtracted from your driver's legal drive window. You can't bill for all of it.
  • Stranded trailers quietly inflate your fleet requirements. The capital cost never shows on a single invoice.
  • Loads that cancel without warning cost drivers. Turnover runs $8–12K per replacement.
  • Modern,fuel-efficient fleets pay the same surcharge rate as inefficient ones — because nobody's sharing consumption data to prove otherwise.
  • The shipper has the signals. No system connects your planning horizon to their operational data.

How It Works
Operations Workflow

Where the signal exists — and where it fails to travel.

Every accessorial originates from a decision point in your workflow where a signal existed but didn't reach the person who needed to act on it. Precursory maps those gaps — then closes them.

01
Order signal exists in ERP

Production delays, volume dips, and SKU shifts register in your ERP and WMS before dispatch. The tender goes out anyway — no one is reading those signals against your carrier's planning horizon.

02
Carrier commits the asset

The driver is dispatched. The truck moves. The carrier has no visibility into the upstream conditions that have already shifted — and no lead time to redirect the asset before deadhead miles accumulate.

03
The fee clock starts

TONU is triggered on cancellation. Detention begins the moment the truck arrives at a congested dock. Per-diem accrues silently on trailers your TMS already shows as idle. The invoice arrives weeks later.
